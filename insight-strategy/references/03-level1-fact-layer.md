# Level 1: Fact Layer

Level 1 answers: **What are people actually saying or what does the evidence
actually show?**

This is the fact layer. Do not explain motives yet. Do not rush into strategy.

## Process

1. Read the evidence pool and group similar evidence items.
2. Identify 6-10 themes or signals when evidence volume allows.
3. Keep weak but potentially interesting signals if they may matter later.
4. Attach raw evidence to every theme.
5. Note source concentration or sample bias.

## Output For Each Theme

- Theme name: 2-5 words
- What people are discussing: 1-2 sentences
- Evidence: 3-4 raw quotes or traceable observations
- Source spread: which source types support this theme
- Confidence: high, medium, low

## Guardrails

- High-frequency words are not themes until connected to context.
- A quote is not representative just because it is vivid.
- Do not remove contradictions. Preserve tensions inside the evidence.
- Keep the original voice where possible, including informal wording.

Level 1 should leave the reader thinking: "I can see the evidence pattern."
