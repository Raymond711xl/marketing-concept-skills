# Concept And Message House Guide

Use this guide after Level 4 has selected an Idea Platform and the user wants
campaign concepts, content territories, communication claims, or execution
directions.

The Idea Platform is the strategic roof for the whole brand or campaign period.
A Concept is a specific expression route under that platform. Each Concept uses
a Message House to keep its claim, support, and proof coherent.

## Relationship

```text
Idea Platform
  -> Concept A -> Message House -> executions
  -> Concept B -> Message House -> executions
  -> Concept C -> Message House -> executions
```

- **Idea Platform**: one strategic platform; answers what the brand should
  stand for and prove.
- **Concept**: a concrete expression territory; answers what this campaign,
  content series, activation, product experience, or touchpoint should say/do.
- **Message House**: the message architecture for one Concept; keeps the claim
  focused and evidence-backed.

Do not let a Concept replace the Idea Platform. Concepts are plural and
execution-facing. The Idea Platform is singular and strategy-facing.

## Message House Structure

For each Concept:

- **Roof**: the single core message or claim of this Concept.
- **Pillars**: 2-3 supporting messages that explain the roof.
- **Foundation**: reasons to believe, product facts, brand behaviors, examples,
  proofs, demonstrations, or source evidence that support the pillars.

If the roof cannot be supported by pillars and foundation, the Concept is weak.
If the pillars do not clearly support the roof, the Message House is unfocused.
If the foundation is mostly adjectives rather than proof, mark the Concept as
provisional or revise it.

## Derivation Steps

1. Start from the selected Idea Platform Record.
2. List possible Concept angles: product, content, experience, event, channel,
   community, partnership, retail, service, or social conversation.
3. For each angle, write one Roof claim.
4. Add 2-3 Pillars that explain or dramatize the Roof.
5. Add Foundation proof from brand facts, product proof, channel behavior, or
   evidence pool items.
6. Complete `assets/templates/concept-record-template.md`.
7. Evaluate concepts for relevance, distinction, credibility, action potential,
   and fit with the Idea Platform.

## Mini Example

Selected Idea Platform:

```text
Because urban skincare users feel caught between self-optimization pressure and
the desire to feel already enough, the brand should stand for returning skincare
to self-care rather than self-assessment, and prove it through simpler formulas
and non-pressure communication behavior.
```

| Concept | Roof | Angle | Pillars | Foundation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Product concept | Fewer ingredients, easier trust | Product | simpler formula; lower decision stress; daily usability | formula list, user comments, product claims |
| Communication concept | We do not hurry you into becoming better | Content | no fear-based comparison; honest product limits; calmer language | detail pages, service scripts, owned content |
| Experience concept | Night skincare as rest | Occasion | evening care; sensory calm; less self-judgment | usage occasion, reviews, routine evidence |

## Concept Quality Checks

- Does it clearly serve the selected Idea Platform?
- Does it have one Roof, not several competing claims?
- Do the Pillars explain the Roof rather than introduce unrelated topics?
- Does the Foundation contain proof, not just tone words?
- Is it meaningfully different from other Concepts under the same platform?
- Can it produce executions across the intended channels?

## Output

Use one `concept-record-template.md` per Concept. Multiple Concept Records can
share the same `Idea Platform source`, but their Roof claims should be distinct.
