# Level 2: Motive Inference

Level 2 answers: **Why might people be saying this? What emotion, need, fear,
or desire is underneath the fact pattern?**

This is an inference layer. Be explicit about uncertainty.

## Process

For each Level 1 theme:

1. Identify the dominant emotion or emotional mix.
2. Infer the underlying need, fear, desire, or social pressure.
3. Distinguish direct evidence from interpretation.
4. Write a human-truth sentence.
5. Assign confidence.

## Human Truth Sentence

Use this structure:

```text
On the surface, they are talking about X, but underneath, they are really
anxious about / longing for Y.
```

Chinese version:

```text
表面上，他们在谈论的是 X，但在底层，他们真正焦虑/渴望的是 Y。
```

## Output For Each Theme

- Theme name
- Dominant emotion
- Psychological need, fear, or desire
- Human truth sentence
- Evidence used
- Confidence: high, medium, low
- Inference notes: what is directly supported vs inferred

Level 2 should leave the reader thinking: "I understand the human pressure
behind the evidence."
