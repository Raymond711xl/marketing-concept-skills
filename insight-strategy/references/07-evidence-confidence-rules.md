# Evidence And Confidence Rules

Use these rules before finalizing any insight or strategy output.

## Evidence Traceability

Every major claim must trace to one or more evidence items. Use source labels,
quotes, links, file names, or evidence IDs.

## Fact vs Inference vs Judgment

Label the layer clearly:

- Level 1: fact layer - what the evidence says
- Level 2: motive inference - why people may say it
- Level 3: cultural judgment - what broader contradiction it suggests
- Level 4: strategic decision - what the brand should choose

## Confidence Labels

Use:

- High: multiple source types, repeated evidence, clear raw support
- Medium: repeated evidence but limited source spread or some ambiguity
- Low: thin evidence, narrow source base, contradictory signals
- Speculative: useful hypothesis with limited direct evidence

## Anti-Hallucination Rules

- Do not fabricate quotes, statistics, URLs, dates, or competitor behavior.
- Do not upgrade a vivid anecdote into a market truth.
- Do not turn brand-owned messaging into consumer truth.
- Do not treat absence of evidence as proof.
- If the evidence pool is not enough, say what is missing.

## Strategic Honesty

If the input package does not contain enough brand truth, product proof,
repeated brand behavior, audience proof, or competitor difference, Level 4 can
still produce candidates but cannot pretend to be final. The final report must
mark the strategy as provisional and recommend a rollback path.
