# Level 3: Cultural Judgment

Level 3 answers: **What larger cultural tension is revealed when multiple human
truths are seen together?**

This is the cultural judgment layer. It moves beyond individual comments or
themes into a broader social contradiction.

## Process

1. Review all Level 2 human truths.
2. Look for cross-theme connections, not one-theme-one-tension mapping.
3. Ask which different themes are secretly pointing to the same unresolved
   cultural pressure.
4. Form 2-4 cultural tensions.
5. Preserve source trails for each tension.

## Cultural Tension Format

For each tension:

- Tension name
- `What is`: the reality, rule, norm, constraint, or current belief people are
  stuck inside
- `What should be`: the desired world, felt ideal, or better social contract
- `Why now`: why this tension is sharper now
- Source themes: Level 1 themes that support it
- Source human truths: Level 2 truths that support it
- Representative evidence
- Confidence and caveats

## Quality Bar

A cultural tension is not a generic pain point. It should expose a gap between
how life currently works and how people feel it ought to work.

Weak:

```text
People want better products.
```

Stronger:

```text
People are asked to optimize every part of life, but they long for choices that
make them feel whole rather than merely efficient.
```

Level 3 should leave the reader thinking: "This is the deeper contradiction the
brand can choose to stand inside."
